# 1Çatı Trailer Production Plan

Status: July 2026  
Purpose: Premium trailer and walkthrough architecture for the 1Çatı client demo.  
Primary market: Turkey. Secondary languages: German and English.  
Target tools: Playwright screen recording, HeyGen avatar / voice, final manual QA.

## Executive Direction

The best structure is not one long product movie.

Use:

- One premium trailer for the landing page and the first client impression.
- A separate role-based walkthrough playlist for real product understanding.

Recommended trailer length:

- Website hero / landing page: 90-120 seconds.
- Client meeting trailer: 2:15-2:45.
- Absolute maximum: 3:30 if the customer is already in a meeting context.

Avoid a 5-minute "trailer". Five minutes should be a guided demo or playlist, not a landing-page trailer.

## Why This Format

Current video marketing benchmarks support short, high-quality explainers and demos:

- Wyzowl 2026 reports that 71% of people see 30 seconds to 2 minutes as the most effective video length.
- Wyzowl also reports that 96% of people have watched an explainer video to understand a product or service, and 89% say video quality affects brand trust.
- Vidyard's benchmark report shows strong growth in AI avatar adoption for sales workflows, but the useful pattern is personalization and human connection, not replacing the product demo with a talking head.
- DataReportal's Turkey 2026 report shows very high video platform reach in Turkey, especially Instagram, YouTube and TikTok. For this market, the trailer must be visually strong, captioned, clear on mobile, and trust-building.

## Turkish Market Psychology

The trailer should feel:

- Trustworthy before futuristic.
- Premium, but not loud.
- Operationally serious, not "AI magic".
- Practical for Turkish property developers: cost control, service quality, owner transparency, reduced chaos.
- International enough for foreign owners, but grounded in Turkish real-estate operations.

The message should avoid:

- Too much English jargon.
- Overpromising "autonomous AI".
- A long avatar monologue.
- Small unreadable UI text.
- Generic SaaS stock-video feeling.

The strongest emotional triggers for this client:

- "No development cost."
- "The facility becomes easier to control."
- "Owners see transparency."
- "The developer looks premium without carrying IT complexity."
- "AI helps, but management remains in control."

## Avatar Strategy

Use the avatar as a host, not as the main content.

Best use:

- Full-screen avatar for the first 6-8 seconds.
- Small picture-in-picture avatar for one transition.
- Full-screen avatar again for the final 8-10 seconds.

Do not use:

- Three minutes of continuous avatar talking.
- Avatar covering important UI.
- Avatar making claims that the product screen does not prove.

The product UI must be the hero.

   - Final closing scene.

5. Use Turkish as the master version.
6. Add subtitles manually or review all auto-subtitles.

8. Use the German script above, not raw machine translation.
9. Export 1080p for website and meeting.
10. Create a shorter 9:16 social version only after the 16:9 master is approved.

## Visual Style

Use:

- Real UI, not generic stock visuals.
- Tight zooms on KPI cards.
- Smooth cut transitions, not excessive effects.
- Clean caption bars.
- Minimal dark/premium overlays.
- One accent color from the 1Çatı brand.
- Short words on screen: "769 daire", "AI risk", "SLA", "Finans", "Rol bazlı erişim".

Avoid:

- Neon sci-fi overload.
- Too many animated stickers.
- Overuse of avatar.
- Fast scrolling.
- Long dashboard shots with no movement.
- Tiny unreadable text.

## Edge Cases And Risks

- Do not show real customer personal data.
- Do not claim production integrations are live if they are UAT/provider dependent.
- Do not imply AI can approve payments, access, refunds or role changes.
- Keep Turkish characters correct: 1Çatı, daire, yönetim, bakım, erişim.
- Validate German pronunciation for 1Çatı. Use phonetic spelling only in the voice field if needed.
- Every translated version must be reviewed, especially offer/pricing lines.
- Add captions because many viewers watch muted.
- Keep screen text large enough for laptop and phone playback.
- Export final in 1080p H.264 MP4 for broad compatibility.
