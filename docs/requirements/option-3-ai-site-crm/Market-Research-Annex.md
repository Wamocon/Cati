## 1. Research Position

The client request should be treated as a full residential operations platform, not a simple CRM. Market research shows that serious competitors already provide property accounting, payments, resident/owner portals, service requests, maintenance workflows, mobile access, communication, reporting, integrations, onboarding and increasingly AI assistance.

The correct business response is to match the client's mandatory scope and add AI, workflow clarity, premium web/PWA UX, stronger reporting, and Turkish-localized service delivery.

## 4. Market Gap Opportunities

The strongest competitive opportunities are:

1. Combine Turkish site management needs with a modern global SaaS UX.
2. Replace vague "service management" with a real service desk/ticketing system.
3. Build a true finance ledger, not a simple balance table.
4. Make debt restrictions configurable, auditable and transparent.
5. Use PWA to deliver mobile access faster than native development.
6. Add AI, but keep AI controlled with human approval.
7. Make the dashboard action-oriented, not only informational.
8. Treat support, migration and training as part of the product offer.
9. Make access integrations auditable and failure-aware.
10. Make Turkish copy, SMS/email, receipts and official-looking statements central to trust.

## 5. Top Five Turkey Player Deep-Dive

These five players are included because they are publicly visible and relevant to the client's requested scope. They should be treated as benchmarks, not as a legally ranked market-share list.

### 5.1 Apsiyon

Observed capabilities:

- Aidat tracking.
- Online bank integrations.
- Card collection.
- Reservation.
- Free website.
- Manager mobile.
- E-mail and SMS.
- Access control products.
- Meter reading and billing.
- AI assistants and digital-assistant positioning.

Implications:

- Our product must include finance, payments, communication and reservation as baseline.
- Access and meter integrations should be planned even if not launched in phase one.
- AI should be useful in real operational scenarios: debt, request triage, reporting and anomaly detection.

### 5.2 Senyonet

Observed capabilities:

- Site, apartment, facility, hotel, office, AVM and management-company positioning.
- Public relations/communication module.
- Finance module.
- Accounting module.
- Security module.
- Requests, announcements, events, reservations and document management.
- Bank, cash and current account visibility.
- Collections, invoicing, budgeting and reporting.
- Visitor, personnel, cargo and vehicle tracking.
- Support and training.

Implications:

- Our platform must support connected operations, not only CRM records.
- Security/visitor workflows should be considered in access/integration planning.
- Support/training should be part of the delivery model.

### 5.3 Yönetimcell

Observed capabilities:

- Aidat tracking.
- Online bank integration.
- Credit card and Masterpass collection.
- Resident account/payment access.
- Reports and Excel exports.
- Multi-site/apartment management.
- Manager mobile app.
- Resident aidat tracking app.
- Staff job-tracking app.

Implications:

- Simplicity and learnability are competitive advantages in Turkey.
- Export and reporting are mandatory for managers and management companies.
- Staff job tracking validates the need for a proper ticket/task module.
- PWA must cover the core value of mobile apps without delaying launch.

### 5.4 Aidatım

Observed capabilities:

- Private assistant.
- Management software.
- Accounting support.
- Legal support.
- Setup and transition help.
- Credit card collection.
- Bank account tracking with smart matching.
- Leave and payroll.
- Monthly/yearly analysis and reports.
- Current account tracking and expense sharing.
- Meter reading and billing.
- E-mail and SMS.
- Account sharing.

Implications:

- Migration, setup and support must be part of the offer.
- Smart reconciliation should be included in finance roadmap.
- Payroll/leave can remain non-goal for launch but should be extensible later.
- Legal review is required for debt, access and collection workflows.

### 5.5 Siteplus

Observed capabilities:

- Integrated facility management.
- Professional site management.
- Private security.
- Cleaning.
- Alarm monitoring.
- Landscaping.
- Pest control and disinfection.
- Technical maintenance and repair.

Implications:

- Our service module must support real field operations.
- Work queues should cover cleaning, security, repair and technical maintenance.
- Media evidence, SLA, staff/contractor assignment and incident tracking are critical.
- The system should work for a professional facility management company, not only resident self-service.

## 6. Edge-Case Coverage Summary

The expanded BRD/TRD now includes edge cases across:

- Multiple owners and multi-flat ownership.
- Tenant permission limits.
- Vacant flats with owner debt.
- Duplicate imports.
- Partial payments and overpayments.
- Delayed/duplicate payment webhooks.
- Deposit shortfall and refund failure.
- Debt threshold changes.
- Emergency maintenance override.
- Service ticket duplicates.
- Media upload failures.
- Booking overlaps.
- Access integration failure.
- SMS/email delivery failure.
- Staff internal notes leaking to residents.
- AI hallucination, role leakage and unauthorized action.
- Document retention and KVKK privacy risks.

The key product rule is that risky cases must be handled by state machines, audit logs and approval workflows, not by informal manual edits.

## 7. Recommended Differentiators For Proposal

### 7.1 Business Differentiators

- Full 769-flat operating model.
- Finance-first architecture.
- Service desk/ticketing module.
- Web/PWA launch instead of slow native app dependency.
- AI manager assistant.
- AI debt-risk and anomaly detection.
- Turkish-first onboarding and support.
- Audit and accountability at every critical action.

### 7.2 UX Differentiators

- Flat matrix by block/floor.
- One-click view of debt status per flat.
- Resident self-service with simple mobile web.
- Staff task mobile view with photo/video reports.
- Manager daily briefing.
- Accountant ledger and export tools.

### 7.3 Technical Differentiators

- API-first modular architecture.
- PostgreSQL ledger.
- RLS/RBAC security.
- Integration adapter layer.
- AI recommendations with human approval.
- PWA-first delivery.
- End-to-end test coverage for mandatory scenarios.
