The Reality

Why property management in Turkey is hard

Real workflows and numbers that Ataberk Estate's managers face every day — amplified by the new rules of 2026.

💬

Leads disappear in messengers

A buyer messages on WhatsApp, Instagram and the website at the same time. After 30 minutes conversion drops by two-thirds. Without a single CRM, the history is lost when the agent leaves.

⏱

30–40% of time is manual admin

Managers spend almost half the day on data entry, payment reconciliation and report preparation instead of working with clients.

🧩

5–7 disconnected tools

CRM, Excel, WhatsApp, email, calendar and cloud drives do not talk to each other. Data is duplicated and goes stale.

🔧

Maintenance requests are forgotten in chats

An urgent leak and cosmetic repair have the same priority. Without before/after photos and history, disputes with owners are inevitable.

⚠️

Fines up to 1,000,000 ₺ for short-term rentals

Law 7464, TÜRSAB licence, KBS/e-GUEST within 24 hours — non-compliance costs hundreds of thousands of lira.

🌍

Foreign owners demand transparency

Time-zone and language gaps. Owners want photo reports, clear accounting and confidence that the property is safe.

📝

EİDS authorization is hard to track

Since 15 February 2026 every sale listing on Sahibinden/Hepsiemlak needs e-Devlet owner authorization. Owners may grant it and still sell directly, creating commission disputes.

🛡

Fake TAPU and fake listings

Forged title deeds, fake contracts and unauthorised agencies still happen. Foreign buyers are especially targeted, so every deal needs structured due diligence.

🛂

Residence rules are tightening

$200,000 minimum property value, SPK-approved appraisal, income/insurance proof and 25% foreigner quota limits in Antalya/Alanya. Every file is different.
