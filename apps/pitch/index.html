The Reality

Why property management in Turkey is hard

Real workflows and risks that Ataberk Estate's managers face every day — amplified by the 2026 regulatory tightening.

💬

Leads disappear in messengers

A buyer messages on WhatsApp, Instagram and the website at the same time. After 30 minutes conversion drops sharply. Without a single CRM, the history is lost when the agent leaves.

⏱

30–40% of time is manual admin

Managers spend almost half the day on data entry, payment reconciliation and report preparation instead of working with clients.

🧩

5–7 disconnected tools

CRM, Excel, WhatsApp, email, calendar and cloud drives do not talk to each other. Data is duplicated and goes stale.

🔧

Maintenance requests are forgotten in chats

An urgent leak and cosmetic repair have the same priority. Without before/after photos and history, disputes with owners are inevitable.

⚠️

Short-term rental fines start at 100,000 ₺

Under Law 7464, unlicensed short-term rentals start at ~100,000 ₺ per property and escalate to 1,000,000 ₺ for repeat violations. Tourism permits and guest reporting are now enforced.

🌍

Foreign owners expect transparency

Time-zone and language gaps. Owners want photo reports, clear accounting and confidence that the property is safe.

📝

EİDS authorization is hard to track

Since 15 February 2026, every electronically published sale listing requires e-Devlet owner authorization via EİDS. Owners may grant and later revoke it, creating commission disputes.

🛡

Fake TAPU and fake listings

Forged title deeds, fake contracts and unauthorised agencies still happen. Foreign buyers are especially targeted, so every deal needs structured due diligence.

🛂

Residence rules are tightening

$200,000 minimum property value for short-term residence permits, SPK-approved appraisal, income/insurance proof, and district-level caps where foreign residents exceed 25% in Antalya/Alanya.
